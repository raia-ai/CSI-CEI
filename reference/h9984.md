# Bolting Data

Two bolt tables are available in the Finglow Software - Inch series bolting with unified screw threads and metric series bolting with ISO metric screw threads. The recommended minimum clearances in these tables are based on TEMA TABLE D-5 [\[26\]](../h9998.htm#0026). The root area is calculated by the program from the effective diameter. The areas given in the following tables are for guidance only. **NOTE -:** The root area calculations does not take into account the diameter of any unthreaded portion of the bolt. The user should select _User defined_ bolts to cater for this.

## ISO METRIC SERIES BOLTING

Metric series bolting is to BS 3643 Parts 1 and 2 - see [\[12\]](../h9998.htm#0012). Screw threads are Coarse Pitch series for bolt sizes up to and including M27, constant 3 mm pitch for sizes from M30 up to M42, and constant 4 mm pitch for sizes M45 and above. The effective diameter used in root area calculation is based on the minor diameter _d_ 3 as defined in BS 3643: Part 1, Appendix A, clause A.3, except for calculations per EN 1591 and EN 13445 where the root area is the _Stress area_ as defined in clause A.3 The data in Table 1 is applicable for any class of thread. All the data is in SI units and is converted if US units are selected.

## INCH SERIES BOLTING

Inch series bolting is to BS 1580 Parts 1 and 2 - see [\[10\]](../h9998.htm#0010) (in accord with the corresponding sections of ANSI B1.1). Screw threads are UNC for bolt sizes up to and including 1 inch diameter, and 8 UN (8 threads per inch) for sizes 1.125 inch diameter and above. The root area is the cross section at minor diameter as tabulated in BS 1580 Table 15 column 8 for UNC threads and Table 20 column 8 for 8 UN threads - see PD 5500, sub-section 3.8.1.4. The root area is calculated from the formula given in NOTE 1 to Table 15, except for calculations to EN 1591 or EN 13445 where the root area is the stress area defined in NOTE 2. NOTE: The root areas for Inch series bolting used in PD 5500 are different from those used in ASME VIII Div.1 (as tabulated in TEMA, TABLE D-5). The data in Table 2 is applicable for any class of thread. All the data is in US units and is converted if SI units are selected.
